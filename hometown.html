WELCOME TO KANJIRAPPALLY

QUEEN OF MALANAD

Where rubber trees stand tall and festivals never fade, Kanjirappally's heart beats in rhythm with its people

Kanjirappally is a town and taluk located in the Kottayam district of Kerala, India, approximately 40 kilometers from Kottayam city/

Nestled at the foothills of the Western Ghats, it is often referred to as the 'Queen of Malanad' and the 'Gateway of Malanad,' highlighting its scenic beauty and cultural significance.

CULTURE AND HERITAGE

Kanjirappally's heritage is a harmonious blend of diverse religious traditions (Syrian Christian, Muslim, and Hindu), rich agricultural practices (especially rubber cultivation), and vibrant cultural expressions through festivals, art forms, and local craftsmanship, reflecting the unique socio-cultural fabric of Kerala’s rural landscape.

ATTRACTONS

    Kanjirappally has several attractions that highlight its natural beauty, cultural significance, and religious diversity. Here are some key attractions to visit in and around the town:

  • Ayyampara-A scenic hilltop offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes, including rubber plantations, valleys, and hills. It's a perfect spot for nature lovers and a short trek to explore the area’s beauty.
  • Rubber Plantations- Kanjirappally is famous for its vast rubber plantations. Visitors can take guided tours to see how rubber is harvested and processed. The lush green plantations also offer scenic views.
  • Spice Plantations- The region around Kanjirappally is also known for its spice cultivation (especially cardamom and pepper). Visiting these plantations provides insight into Kerala’s spice heritage and offers a chance to buy fresh spices.
  • Kottathavalam- A natural rock formation that is an excellent spot for trekking and sightseeing.The Kottathavalam provides a great view of the surrounding valleys and rubber plantations.
  • Vagamon- Although not directly in Kanjirappally, Vagamon is a nearby hill station (about 30-40 km away). Known for its lush green meadows, tea gardens, and cool climate, it's perfect for a day trip to enjoy nature, trekking, and scenic views.
  • Illikkal Kallu- A rock formation located near Kanjirappally, Illikkal Kallu is famous for its unique shape and breathtaking views. It's a popular spot for trekkers and nature enthusiasts.

These attractions offer a mix of nature, culture, spirituality, and history, making Kanjirappally a unique and enriching destination.

Kanjirappally is not just a place; it's a feeling — where every moment is wrapped in green, faith, and family.
